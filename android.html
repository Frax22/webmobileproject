History

  • 2008Android 1.0

    This was the first commercial version of Android, released in 2008. It featured a simple user interface and support for basic features such as phone calls, text messaging, and email.

  • 2009Android 1.5 Cupcake

    This version introduced a number of new features, including a virtual keyboard, home screen widgets, and support for video recording.

  • 2009Android 1.6 Donut

    This version introduced a number of improvements, including support for larger screen sizes, improved search functionality, and better camera support.

  • 2009Android 2.0/2.1 Eclair

    Introduced a number of new features, including Google Maps Navigation, live wallpapers, and support for multiple Google accounts.

  • 2010Android 2.2 Froyo

    This version introduced a number of performance improvements, including support for faster web browsing, better app management, and the ability to install apps to an SD card.

  • 2010Android 2.3 Gingerbread

    Support for Near Field Communication (NFC), a redesigned virtual keyboard, and improved power management.

  • 2011Android 3.0 Honeycomb

    This version was designed specifically for tablet devices and introduced a number of new features, including a redesigned user interface, support for multi-tasking, and a new virtual keyboard.

  • 2011Android 4.0 Ice Cream Sandwich

    New virtual assistant (Google Now), a redesigned user interface, and support for wireless printing.

  • 2012Android 4.1 Jelly Bean

    Improved notifications, and the ability to swipe between home screens.

  • 2013Android 4.4 KitKat

    Introduced a redesigned user interface, support for Google Cloud Print, and the ability to use your device as a TV remote control.

  • 2014Android 5.0 Lollipop

    Redesigned user interface, support for multiple user accounts, and improved battery life.

  • 2015Android 6.0 Marshmallow

    support for Google Now on Tap, improved battery life, and the ability to grant or deny app permissions on a per-app basis.

  • 2016Android 7.0 Nougat

    Support for split-screen multitasking, improved notifications, and the ability to quickly switch between apps.

  • 2017Android 8.0 Oreo

    Introduced support for picture-in-picture mode, improved battery life, and the ability to snooze notifications.

  • 2018Android 9.0 Pie

    New gesture-based navigation system, improved battery life, and the ability to use your phone as a digital wellbeing tool.

  • 2019Android 10

    Support for a system-wide dark mode, improved privacy controls, and the ability to share your Wi-Fi network with others.

  • 2020Android 11

    Introduces "conversations" notifications that can be presented in pop-up overlays known as "bubbles" when supported by apps. Also includes a built-in screen recorder and the voice control system is capable of recognizing screen context.

  • 2021Android 12

    Improved privacy controls, a new material design, and the ability to schedule when apps can access your microphone and camera.

  • 2022Android 13

    Improved performance, enhanced security features, and the ability to easily manage and organize your apps and notifications.

  • 2023Android 14

    Much improved lock/home screen customization, simplified privacy settings and data preferences.
